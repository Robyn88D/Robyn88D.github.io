What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns—warmer, wetter, colder or drier. In simple terms, it’s extreme weather change. It can either overheat or super cool the Earth. Scientists typically state that climate change started in the late part of the 19th century. It started becoming a problem in 1988 according to “Welcome to the United Nations”. How it happens: Climate Change happens when gasses enter the upper atmosphere. This can happen due to volcanic eruptions, changes in the food chain, or more famously, us humans. These gasses can make the earth colder by blocking out the sun (eg, volcanic eruptions,) or make the earth warmer by trapping heat like a blanket.

What will happen if we don’t take action?

Climate change will cause average global temperatures to increase beyond 37.5°F, and will adversely affect every ecosystem.

How YOU can help + Contact Page

Climate change is a huge issue, and we need all the help that we can get, including yours! There are a million things that you do to help save this planet! Even the smallest things in your day to day life can help make a big impact. Some examples are…

• Save energy at home

Turn off the lights when you aren’t using them, unplugging after use, low-energy lighting, these are just a few examples of what you can do to save energy at home. For more information and tips click here! And remember climate change is a task for all of us. It concerns all of us. No one can do it all alone—but we can do it together.

• Walk, bike, or take public transportation (such as a bus)

On the road, most vehicles you see burn diesel or gasoline. Walking or riding a bike instead will reduce greenhouse gas emissions, as well as help your health and fitness. For longer distances, consider taking public transport such as a train or bus.

• Throw away less food (don’t waste food)

When you throw food away, you're also wasting the resources and energy that were used to grow, produce, package, and transport it. The food you wasted often rots in a landfill, it produces methane, a powerful greenhouse gas.
